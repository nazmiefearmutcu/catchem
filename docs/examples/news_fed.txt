The Federal Reserve raised its benchmark interest rate by 25 basis points
on Wednesday, citing persistent inflation pressures and a tight labor
market. Treasury yields jumped after the decision: the 2-year yield
climbed 8 bps to 4.78% and the 10-year added 5 bps to 4.41%.

Chair Powell said the central bank remains data-dependent and would
continue to assess incoming inflation and labor-market readings before
deciding on further moves. He cautioned that a return to the 2% target
"may take longer than previously expected."

Equities sold off on the news. Apple (AAPL) fell 2.1% and Microsoft
(MSFT) lost 1.8%. The S&P 500 closed down 0.9%. The dollar strengthened
broadly, with EUR/USD slipping below 1.06.

Bond markets are now pricing roughly two more hikes by year-end, up from
one before today's meeting. Analysts at major banks said the hawkish
tone leaves the door open for additional tightening if core CPI does
not cool.
