Nvidia (NVDA) reported fourth-quarter results on Wednesday that easily
beat Wall Street expectations and raised its outlook for the current
quarter, sending shares up more than 7% in extended trading.

Revenue came in at $26.4 billion, against consensus estimates near
$24.6 billion. Data-center revenue, the segment that includes the H200
and Blackwell accelerator families, rose 92% year over year to
$19.8 billion. Gaming revenue grew 14% to $2.9 billion.

Chief Executive Jensen Huang said demand for accelerated computing
"continues to outstrip what we can deliver" and reaffirmed that
Blackwell shipments are now ramping in volume. Guidance for the next
quarter implied roughly $30 billion in revenue, well above analyst
forecasts.

Operating margin expanded to 65.5% from 58.2% a year ago. Free cash
flow nearly doubled to $15.5 billion. The company also announced a
$50 billion share-repurchase authorization.

Suppliers and adjacent names rallied with NVDA in after-hours trade.
AMD added 3.1%, Broadcom (AVGO) was up 2.4%, and TSMC ADRs (TSM) gained
1.9%. The S&P 500 futures pointed roughly +0.5% higher on the print.
