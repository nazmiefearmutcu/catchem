Brent crude jumped more than 4% on Tuesday after OPEC+ agreed to extend
voluntary output cuts of 2.2 million barrels per day through the
second quarter, citing fragile demand and elevated inventories in OECD
storage. WTI crude (CL=F) traded above $84 a barrel for the first time
since October.

Saudi Arabia's energy ministry said the group remains "ready to take
further measures if market conditions warrant," language analysts read
as a willingness to deepen cuts at the next ministerial meeting. The
extension keeps roughly 5.9 million bpd of OPEC+ supply off the market
when combined with prior pledges.

Energy equities rallied. Exxon Mobil (XOM) gained 2.6%, Chevron (CVX)
added 2.3%, and Shell (SHEL) closed up 2.1%. The S&P 500 energy index
was the day's best-performing sector, up 1.9%.

Goldman Sachs lifted its Q2 Brent forecast to $90 from $85 and warned
that any unplanned outage in the Middle East could push prices through
$95. The dollar weakened slightly against commodity-linked currencies;
USD/CAD slipped 0.4%.

The move added to inflation worries already running through bond
markets. The 10-year Treasury yield climbed 6 basis points to 4.46%,
and Fed-funds futures trimmed bets on near-term rate cuts. Inflation
breakevens widened by 4-5 bps across the curve.
