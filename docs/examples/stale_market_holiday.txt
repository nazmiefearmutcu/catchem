Major U.S. equity markets were closed on Monday in observance of a
federal holiday. The New York Stock Exchange and Nasdaq remained dark,
along with the bond market, which closed early on Friday.

European trading was light. The FTSE 100 finished essentially flat at
7,612, while the DAX gained 0.1% and the CAC 40 slipped 0.2%. Volumes
across all three exchanges were less than half of recent twenty-day
averages, traders said, with the U.S. session offline.

Asian markets had closed mostly mixed in earlier trading. The Nikkei
225 ended down 0.4%, the Hang Seng was little changed, and Shanghai's
composite added 0.1%. The yen drifted toward 158 per dollar on thin
flows.

Crude oil edged lower in electronic trading, with Brent settling near
$82 a barrel. Gold traded around $2,360. Bitcoin held above $66,000
on light overnight volume.

Markets reopen Tuesday with U.S. retail-sales data and producer-price
revisions on the morning calendar.
